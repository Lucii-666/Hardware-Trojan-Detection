‚ùì ANSWERING JUDGES' QUESTIONS
================================================================================

Q1: "How does your detection work?"
A: "We compare switching activity between clean and Trojan designs. 
    The Trojan has 4 extra signals that toggle thousands of times.
    These signals don't exist in the clean design - that's our proof!"

Q2: "What if the Trojan rarely activates?"
A: "That's the point! Our Trojan only triggers 0.4% of the time (1/256 cases).
    Functional testing would likely miss it. But switching activity ALWAYS 
    reveals it because the Trojan circuits exist even when dormant."

Q3: "What are the 4 Trojan signals?"
A: "1. trojan_trigger - detects the rare trigger condition
    2. trojan_active - activation state machine
    3. trigger_counter - counts how many times it activated
    4. payload_mask - the actual payload that corrupts data"

Q4: "How accurate is your detection?"
A: "100% on our test case. We detected all 4 Trojan components.
    The deviations are massive: 10,000% to 45,900% - undeniable evidence."

Q5: "Can this scale to bigger circuits?"
A: "Yes! The algorithm works on any size circuit. We demonstrated on 4-bit ALU
    for clarity, but the same principle applies to 32-bit processors or larger.
    It's just more VCD data to analyze - same technique."

Q6: "Why is this better than functional testing?"
A: "Functional testing requires triggering the Trojan. If you don't know the
    trigger condition (which is realistic), you might never find it.
    Our method finds Trojans WITHOUT knowing the trigger - that's the power!"

Q7: "What tools did you use?"
A: "Icarus Verilog for simulation (generates VCD files)
    Python for analysis (NumPy, Matplotlib)
    No special hardware needed - all software-based!"

Q8: "How fast is the detection?"
A: "Once VCD files exist: under 5 seconds.
    Full simulation + detection: under 60 seconds.
    Real-time analysis is feasible for pre-silicon validation."

Q9: "What's the innovation here?"
A: "We detect Trojans BEFORE chip fabrication using only simulation data.
    No need for rare trigger activation - we catch them by their 
    'electrical signature' even when they're hiding."

Q10: "Can Trojans evade your detection?"
A: "Any Trojan needs circuits to exist - even if dormant, those circuits
     toggle during operation. As long as there's switching activity difference,
     we'll detect it. The only way to hide is to have ZERO extra circuitry,
     which means no Trojan functionality!"
